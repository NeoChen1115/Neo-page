# Neo-page
1.Objective:​ To deepen understanding by surveying recent research. Teams must synthesize findings on:
Core ideas and system designs.
Empirical evidence and evaluation methods.
Challenges and limitations.
Future research directions.
2.Team Formation:
Teams of 3-4 students.
A team coordinator must be nominated.
Membership is fixed after a registration deadline.
3.Task Specification:​ Teams must select one​ topic and:
Survey at least 10 representative research papers.
Create a clear taxonomy (categorization) of methods.
Compare different approaches using consistent criteria (e.g., accuracy, scalability, cost).
Identify key challenges and future directions.
4.Topic Options:​ Teams choose one direction. The main options are:
Option A: Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) for Big Data​ (covers system design, evaluation, vector databases, GraphRAG, and security).
Option B: Text-to-SQL with LLMs​ (covers methods, evaluation, enterprise concerns, and database engineering applications).
Option C: Other Big Data x LLM Topics​ (allows for a related topic of the team's choice, subject to approval).
5.Deliverables:
Survey Report:​ A minimum 2500-word written report reviewing at least 10 papers, including a taxonomy, comparisons, and a discussion of future directions. It must adhere to academic integrity standards (AI can only polish language, not generate content).
Presentation:​ A 7-minute, in-class presentation supported by 5-10 slides, covering the same key elements as the report.
